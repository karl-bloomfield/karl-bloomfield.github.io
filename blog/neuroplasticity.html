Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

1. Research Neuroplasticity Research: what-is-neuroplasticity what-is-brain-plasticity brain-plasticity

Being aware that the brain is malleable, enables students to be unlocked by previous teaching methods and engage with learning. By acknowledging that learning is possible by any one of any age, practising activities to create new neural roadways, and shape a new skill superhighway. Push out of your comfort zone to form new neural connections that will strengthen learning.

2. Research Growth Mindset . Research: growth-mindset growth mindset characteristics benefits growth vs fixed mindset

Growth mindset is process where language roadblocks, like not being able to learn something because you don’t have ‘talent’ or are not ‘gifted’, are pointed at positive acknowledgements and language so that all students are being able to learn and grow. The power of positive thinking is very powerful. Embrace learning, working hard, welcoming challenges, taking feedback constructively, failing forward. I have been following an oxymoron of ‘Get rid of the negative’ since I started, to look at the positive in work and not the negative of failure. Failure is positive, and helps you learn.

3. Blend with your learning plan and strategies

Positive reinforcement, remove any negative language and understanding that the brain itself will learn as you challenge yourself, aids in a much happier mindset to work through difficult and constantly changing challenges. I will endeavour to challenge myself in the work in undertake.
